Atal Arthashayya Yojana




Nanded: The previous government had launched the Atal Arthasahya Yojana to boost the agro-processing industry. 118 organizations from the district submitted applications. Out of which project was sanctioned to 59 organizations. Out of these, 21 institutions applied for loans at their own expense. However, the plan has stalled due to the transition of power in the state in the meantime.

The Cabinet of the then Coalition Government in its meeting held on 18th November 2018 decided to implement a new scheme called Atal Arthasahayya Yojana for Co-operative Societies to promote co-operative society projects. In this, a fund of Rs. 490 crore was provided for the implementation of this scheme in 355 talukas of the state. A fund of Rs 22 crore 8 lakh was sanctioned for 16 talukas in Nanded district.

About 200 innovative co-operative societies in Atkali

district have registered with the District Deputy Registrar under the scheme. It was initially said that there would be a project worth Rs 40 lakh. However, due to the increase in the number of institutions, the project was brought to twenty lakhs. 118 organizations in the district applied to the District Deputy Registrar's Office for this scheme. In the scrutiny, projects of 59 organizations were approved. Out of these, 21 societies have applied for a loan of Rs. However, in the meantime, the BJP-Shiv Sena alliance government came to power in the state and a grand alliance government was formed. Even after this, as the cabinet of this government has not been expanded, this plan has come to a standstill.
